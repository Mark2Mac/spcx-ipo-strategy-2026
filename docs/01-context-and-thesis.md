## 3. The structural anomalies (core of the short thesis)

1. **Accelerated lockup**: instead of the standard 180 days, insiders (Musk excluded) may sell **20% of holdings 2 days after the first quarterly report** (~August 2026). A further 10% unlocks if the stock holds +30% above IPO price for 5 of 10 consecutive sessions. Then 7% tranches at regular intervals through day 135.
2. **Accelerated index inclusion**: Nasdaq-100 after only 15 trading days (standard: 3 months) → passive funds forced to buy ~$30B → artificial price support in the first weeks. Note: S&P confirmed it will NOT change its own inclusion rules (debunking part of the Reddit doomerism).
3. **Retail constraints**: Fidelity (US) $2,000 minimum with anti-flipping penalties up to a lifetime IPO ban. In the EU, secondary-market access only.

## 6. Resulting operational thesis

1. **Do not buy the debut**: price at peak hype, option IV at peak, artificial passive support incoming that inflates it further.
2. **The long-short window is August**: first earnings (showing the xAI burn) + 20% insider unlock. Precedents: Rivian -20% in one day on Ford's sale notice; Uber -40% from IPO price at lockup expiry.
3. **Long exposure only via quality proxies** (GOOGL, ~5% SpaceX stake) until SPCX approaches fair value.
4. **The calendar is the edge, not the direction**: artificial support days 1-30, structural fragility days 60-135.

## 7. Risks to the thesis (steel-manning the bull)

- 4x oversubscribed demand: the initial squeeze can run for months (see Tesla 2020, Palantir 2024).
- Starlink growing ~2x/year: at that pace the multiple compresses on its own.
- Musk at 82%: small float = scarcity = irrational prices sustainable for long stretches.
- Nasdaq-100 inclusion (~Jul 3) and levered ETFs can produce a second rally in July.
- "Markets can stay irrational longer than you can stay solvent" — hence ONLY defined-risk structures.
